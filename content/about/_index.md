The IBL Core Engineering Team is a central group of scientific engineers dedicated to enabling large-scale, collaborative neuroscience. Building on the International Brain Laboratory’s model of open, distributed science, the Core brings together expertise in data, software, and infrastructure to support research across laboratories worldwide.

Originally developed to support a coordinated, large-scale effort across experimental labs, the engineering core played a key role in standardizing data collection, enabling large-scale processing, and integrating analysis across projects. As the collaboration evolved to include a broader set of smaller, investigator-led projects, the Core developed deep expertise in supporting diverse scientific workflows and requirements.

Today, the IBL Core is extending this experience beyond its original scope, partnering with new projects and research groups across neuroscience. It continues to evolve as a collaborative engineering partner, working closely with scientists to design, implement, and scale modern, reproducible research workflows.

## What we do

The core team develops and maintains the technical foundations that make collaborative neuroscience possible:

* Data infrastructure & access : Building tools for standardized data ingestion, organization, and access.
* Processing pipelines : Developing robust, scalable workflows for electrophysiology, imaging, behavior, and video.
* Cloud & web platforms :
Creating shared infrastructure and web-based tools.
* Visualization & software : Designing user interfaces, applications, and libraries.
* Hardware & acquisition support : Providing guidance and solutions for experimental setups and data acquisition systems.
* Training & community :Supporting the broader community through workshops, documentation, and open standards for best practices.


## How we work

At the heart of the engineering core is a commitment to collaboration and open science. We work in partnership with research groups, integrating their data, scaling their analyses, and co-developing tools that are shared openly with the community.

Our approach reflects the broader IBL philosophy: tackling complex scientific questions requires coordinated efforts, shared infrastructure, and a culture of transparency and reproducibility.

## Funding sources

The IBL Core is supported by a new 5-year grant from the Simons Foundation, starting January 1, 2026, enabling the expansion of its scientific and technical activities. In parallel, a 3-year Wellcome Trust grant, beginning April 1, 2026, supports outreach and community engagement efforts. In addition, a number of ongoing projects, supported by the NIH, are expected to conclude in 2026–2027.
